01 / THE SHAPE CHANGE

You're renting tokens, not infrastructure

This single distinction explains every keep/trim decision below. The AI Factory is a private-cloud PaaS: it vends infrastructure to enterprises who bring their own workloads. Inference is the inverse.

HPE AI Factory is built to…
Sell infrastructure to many tenants

Each tenant provisions VMs, catalogs, environments. Tenancy means isolated infrastructure. Hence Morpheus, the multi-tenant IaaS manager, hypervisor, service catalogs, cloud connectors.

vs
Run one workload for many consumers

You operate one service — model serving — at scale. Tenancy means an API key, a quota, a rate limit. It's stateless, horizontally scaled, and billed per token. No VMs handed out.

03 / WHERE COMPLEXITY GOES

The serving-economics layer

Complexity doesn't vanish when you trim the PaaS — it moves up here, and gets sharper. Throughput-per-GPU is your gross margin, so this is the real engineering.

SERVING OPTIMIZATION

Continuous batching, paged-attention/KV-cache, quantization, speculative decoding via vLLM / TensorRT-LLM / NIM. Directly sets tokens/sec/GPU.

GPU ECONOMICS

Bin-packing, fractional GPU, and autoscale-to-zero for cold models (Run.ai helps). Idle GPUs are pure cost.

API GATEWAY

OpenAI-compatible endpoint, SSE streaming, API keys, rate limits, multi-model routing, per-token metering. This is the product surface.

CACHING

Prompt cache + semantic cache. Cuts repeat-compute cost and tail latency — a margin lever, not a nicety.

MODEL CATALOG

Curated models with versions, deprecation, and per-model pricing. Light, but customer-visible.

METERING → BILLING

Per-token rating, quotas, usage dashboards, invoicing. Metering exists in the stack; rating/billing does not.

04 / SOVEREIGNTY DELTA

The sovereignty story gets simpler — and one bit gets harder

Because you're usually not holding a customer's corpus at rest, the residency surface shrinks. But transient data leaves a trail.

Easier No corpus at rest

Data is transient in the request. No customer vector DB, no ingestion classifier, no per-tenant data plane to keep UK-resident. The whole data-provenance problem from the platform doc mostly disappears.

Harder Prompts & completions leave a trail

Logs, traces, caches and abuse-monitoring capture prompts and outputs. Their residency + retention is now the sovereignty question. Offer a UK-only logging path and a contractual zero-retention mode. Still pin models in-region and deny egress.

05 / BOTTOM LINE

When the full stack is justified

Start thin; the factory earns its keep later.

For pure inference, run bare-metal K8s + NIM/vLLM + Run.ai + your gateway — and skip most of the PaaS. The full HPE control plane stops being overkill the moment you move up-market to the managed sovereign platform (RAG, fine-tuning, per-tenant data, self-service environments) from the companion doc. That's what Morpheus, multi-tenancy and service catalogs were built for. Inference-as-a-service is the beachhead; the factory is the destination.
